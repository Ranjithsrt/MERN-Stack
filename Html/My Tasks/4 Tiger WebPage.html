TIGER

ABOUT TIGERS

Tigeris a famous and energetic character from *Winnie the Pooh*, known for his bouncy personality and positive attitude. He loves to jump around on his spring-like tail, always spreading joy and excitement. Tigger often exclaims, **"The wonderful thing about Tiggers is Tiggers are wonderful things!"** He believes he is unique and the best at everything, even when he isn’t. His enthusiasm sometimes gets him into trouble, but he always means well. With his bright orange fur and black stripes, Tigger is one of the most recognizable and loved characters in the Hundred Acre Wood.

DIET

Tiger are carnivorous predators that primarily hunt large animals such as deer, wild boar, and buffalo. They rely on stealth and strength to ambush their prey, using powerful jaws and sharp teeth to deliver a fatal bite. Occasionally, they may eat smaller animals, birds, or even fish. Tigers can consume up to 40 kg of meat in one meal but may go days without eating. They usually hunt alone at night, stalking their prey before launching a quick and deadly attack. Their diet depends on the availability of prey in their habitat, which ranges from dense forests to grasslands.

HABITAT AND DISTRIBUTION

Tiger live in a variety of habitats, including dense forests, mangroves, grasslands, and even snowy regions. They are mainly found in Asia, with populations in India, Russia,China , and Southeast Asia . Tigers prefer areas with abundant prey, water sources, and dense cover for hunting. Bengal tigers are commonly found in India, while Siberian tigers live in cold Russian forests. Sumatran tigers inhabit tropical rainforests. Habitat destruction and poaching have greatly reduced their range, forcing conservation efforts to protect them. National parks and reserves play a crucial role in preserving Tiger populations and preventing extinction.
